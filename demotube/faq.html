Technical Support

For a smooth learning experience, we recommend a minimum internet speed of 5 Mbps for standard definition video (720p) and at least 10 Mbps for high definition content (1080p). If your connection is slower, you can manually select a lower video quality in the player settings — even 2 Mbps is sufficient for 480p streaming. DemoTube's video player also uses adaptive bitrate streaming, which automatically adjusts quality based on your current bandwidth so you experience minimal buffering.
DemoTube is fully supported on all modern browsers, including Google Chrome (recommended), Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, Apple Safari, and Opera. We recommend keeping your browser updated to the latest version for the best performance and security. Internet Explorer is not supported. For mobile users, the platform works seamlessly in Chrome for Android and Safari for iOS. If you encounter any display issues, try clearing your browser cache or switching to Chrome.
If a video isn't playing properly, there are a few quick fixes to try. First, refresh the page and check your internet connection speed. If the issue persists, clear your browser's cache and cookies, then reload the page. Try disabling any browser extensions or ad blockers, as these can sometimes interfere with our video player. Switching to a different browser (e.g., Chrome) often resolves playback issues. If none of these steps help, please report the problem via our support page and include your browser version and operating system details.
We make it easy to report technical problems so we can fix them quickly. Visit our Contact page and fill out the support form, selecting "Technical Issue" as the category. Please include as much detail as possible — your browser, operating system, the course name, and a description of what happened. Screenshots or screen recordings are especially helpful. Our technical support team responds to all reports within 24 business hours and will keep you updated on the resolution progress.

Certificates

Yes! Upon completing 100% of a course — all lessons, quizzes, and any required assignments — DemoTube automatically generates a personalized Certificate of Completion. The certificate includes your name, the course title, the instructor's name, the completion date, and a unique verification ID. You can download it as a high-resolution PDF, print it, or share it digitally. Certificates are available for both free and paid courses, so your hard work is always recognized and documented.
DemoTube certificates are increasingly recognized by employers across the technology, creative, and business industries as evidence of self-driven learning and practical skill development. Each certificate carries a unique verification URL that hiring managers can use to confirm its authenticity online. While our certificates are not formally accredited by government bodies like a university degree, many of our students have used them to land jobs, promotions, and freelance contracts at companies including startups and Fortune 500 firms. Pairing your certificate with a strong portfolio project significantly increases its value to employers.
Sharing your DemoTube certificate on LinkedIn is simple and takes just a few seconds. From your course dashboard, click "View Certificate" and then select the "Add to LinkedIn" button — this will take you directly to LinkedIn's "Add License & Certification" section, with most fields pre-filled for you. Enter the completion date, add the certificate URL for verification, and click Save. Your new certification will appear on your LinkedIn profile under the "Licenses & Certifications" section, visible to recruiters and connections. You can also download the certificate PDF and attach it to job applications or email signatures.
